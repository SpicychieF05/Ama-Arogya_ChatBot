DATE: September 20, 2025
FOCUS: Common Health Problems in Odisha State, India

================================================================================
                                 OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This document outlines the comprehensive disease dataset requirements to optimize
the Rasa-based health chatbot for Odisha's specific health challenges. The 
datasets should cover regional health patterns, multilingual support (English,
Hindi, Odia), and culturally appropriate responses.

TARGET AUDIENCE: Rural and semi-urban populations of Odisha
LANGUAGES: English, Hindi, Odia (Oriya)
DEPLOYMENT: WhatsApp, SMS, Web interface

================================================================================
                            PRIORITY DISEASE CATEGORIES
================================================================================

1. VECTOR-BORNE DISEASES (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
   ========================================
   
   Rationale: Odisha is endemic for multiple vector-borne diseases due to 
   climate, geography, and monsoon patterns.

   A. MALARIA
   ----------
   Intent: ask_malaria
   Training Examples:
   - English: "I have fever with chills", "malaria symptoms", "mosquito bite fever"
   - Hindi: "बुखार और कंपकंपी है", "ठंड लगकर बुखार", "मलेरिया के लक्षण"
   - Odia: "ମଲେରିଆ ଲକ୍ଷଣ", "ଥଣ୍ଡା ଲାଗି ଜ୍ବର", "ମଶାକ କାମୁଡ଼ା ଜ୍ବର"
   
   Key Symptoms: Intermittent fever, chills, sweating, body ache
   Response Focus: Immediate care, when to seek help, prevention
   
   B. DENGUE
   ---------
   Intent: ask_dengue
   Training Examples:
   - English: "severe headache with fever", "dengue symptoms", "body pain fever"
   - Hindi: "तेज़ बुखार और सिरदर्द", "डेंगू के लक्षण", "शरीर में दर्द"
   - Odia: "ଡେଙ୍ଗୁ ଜ୍ବର", "ତୀବ୍ର ମାଥା ବଥା", "ଶରୀର ଦୁଖ"
   
   Key Symptoms: High fever, severe headache, muscle pain, bleeding
   Response Focus: Platelet monitoring, hydration, emergency signs
   
   C. CHIKUNGUNYA
   --------------
   Intent: ask_chikungunya
   Training Examples:
   - English: "joint pain and fever", "swollen joints", "muscle pain fever"
   - Hindi: "जोड़ों में दर्द", "मांसपेशियों में दर्द", "बुखार के साथ दर्द"
   - Odia: "ଗଣ୍ଠି ବଥା", "ମାଂସପେଶୀ ଦୁଖ", "ଜ୍ବର ସହ ଦୁଖ"
   
   Key Symptoms: Joint pain, fever, rash, muscle ache
   Response Focus: Pain management, mobility, long-term care

2. WATER-BORNE DISEASES (HIGH PRIORITY)
   ====================================
   
   Rationale: Monsoon flooding, contaminated water sources, poor sanitation
   
   A. DIARRHEAL DISEASES
   ---------------------
   Intent: ask_diarrhea
   Training Examples:
   - English: "loose motions", "watery stools", "diarrhea dehydration"
   - Hindi: "दस्त हो रहा है", "पेट खराब है", "पानी जैसा मल"
   - Odia: "ପେଟ ଖରାପ", "ତରଳ ମଳ", "ଅତିସାର"
   
   Sub-categories: Acute diarrhea, Dysentery, Cholera
   Response Focus: ORS preparation, hydration, when to hospitalize
   
   B. TYPHOID
   ----------
   Intent: ask_typhoid
   Training Examples:
   - English: "prolonged fever", "typhoid symptoms", "stomach pain fever"
   - Hindi: "लगातार बुखार", "टाइफाइड के लक्षण", "पेट दर्द बुखार"
   - Odia: "ଟାଇଫଏଡ ଲକ୍ଷଣ", "ଲଗାତାର ଜ୍ବର", "ପେଟ ଦୁଖ"
   
   Key Symptoms: Step-ladder fever, rose spots, weakness
   Response Focus: Antibiotic compliance, nutrition, complications

3. RESPIRATORY DISEASES (HIGH PRIORITY)
   ====================================
   
   Rationale: Industrial pollution, mining dust, seasonal variations
   
   A. TUBERCULOSIS
   ---------------
   Intent: ask_tuberculosis
   Training Examples:
   - English: "persistent cough", "blood in cough", "night sweats"
   - Hindi: "लगातार खांसी", "खांसी में खून", "रात को पसीना"
   - Odia: "ଲଗାତାର କାଶ", "କାଶରେ ରକ୍ତ", "ରାତିରେ ଗୁଇ"
   
   Key Symptoms: Chronic cough, hemoptysis, weight loss, night sweats
   Response Focus: DOTS program, contact screening, stigma reduction
   
   B. PNEUMONIA
   ------------
   Intent: ask_pneumonia
   Training Examples:
   - English: "difficulty breathing", "chest pain cough", "rapid breathing"
   - Hindi: "सांस लेने में तकलीफ", "छाती में दर्द", "तेज़ सांस"
   - Odia: "ଶ୍ବାସ କଷ୍ଟ", "ଛାତି ଦୁଖ", "ଦ୍ରୁତ ଶ୍ବାସ"
   
   Key Symptoms: Breathing difficulty, chest pain, fever
   Response Focus: Vaccination, early treatment, danger signs

4. NUTRITIONAL DEFICIENCIES (MEDIUM-HIGH PRIORITY)
   ================================================
   
   Rationale: Rural poverty, food insecurity, dietary habits
   
   A. ANEMIA
   ---------
   Intent: ask_anemia
   Training Examples:
   - English: "feeling weak tired", "pale skin nails", "iron deficiency"
   - Hindi: "कमजोरी और थकान", "पीला चेहरा", "खून की कमी"
   - Odia: "ଦୁର୍ବଳତା", "ପତଳା ରଙ୍ଗ", "ରକ୍ତହୀନତା"
   
   Key Symptoms: Fatigue, pallor, weakness, breathlessness
   Response Focus: Iron-rich foods, supplementation, screening
   
   B. MALNUTRITION (PEDIATRIC)
   ---------------------------
   Intent: ask_malnutrition
   Training Examples:
   - English: "child not gaining weight", "stunted growth", "frequent infections"
   - Hindi: "बच्चा कमजोर है", "वजन नहीं बढ़ रहा", "बार बार बीमार"
   - Odia: "ପିଲା ଦୁର୍ବଳ", "ଓଜନ ବୃଦ୍ଧି ନାହିଁ", "ବାରମ୍ବାର ପୀଡ଼ିତ"
   
   Key Signs: Weight loss, growth retardation, infections
   Response Focus: Nutritional rehabilitation, ICDS programs

5. MATERNAL & CHILD HEALTH (HIGH PRIORITY)
   ========================================
   
   Rationale: High MMR and IMR in rural Odisha
   
   A. PREGNANCY COMPLICATIONS
   --------------------------
   Intent: ask_pregnancy_complications
   Training Examples:
   - English: "bleeding during pregnancy", "high BP pregnancy", "baby movement decreased"
   - Hindi: "गर्भावस्था में खून", "हाई BP प्रेगनेंसी", "बच्चे की हलचल कम"
   - Odia: "ଗର୍ଭାବସ୍ଥାରେ ରକ୍ତସ୍ରାବ", "ଉଚ୍ଚ ରକ୍ତଚାପ", "ପିଲା ଚଳନ କମ"
   
   Key Issues: Bleeding, hypertension, reduced fetal movement
   Response Focus: Emergency referral, ANC importance, danger signs
   
   B. NEWBORN CARE
   ---------------
   Intent: ask_newborn_care
   Training Examples:
   - English: "newborn not feeding", "baby fever", "jaundice newborn"
   - Hindi: "नवजात दूध नहीं पी रहा", "बच्चे को बुखार", "पीलिया"
   - Odia: "ନବଜାତକ କ୍ଷୀର ପିଉନାହିଁ", "ପିଲାର ଜ୍ବର", "ପିତ୍ତଳ ରୋଗ"
   
   Key Issues: Feeding problems, infections, jaundice
   Response Focus: Breastfeeding support, vaccination, danger signs

   - Odia: "ମାନସିକ ଚାପ", "ଉଦାସୀନତା", "ନିଦ୍ରା ସମସ୍ୟା"

================================================================================
                            IMPLEMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

1. NLU TRAINING DATA STRUCTURE
   ============================

   For each disease category, create:
   
   A. Intent Definition
   B. Multi-language Training Examples (minimum 50 per language)
   C. Entity Recognition (symptoms, duration, severity)
   D. Response Templates with:
      - Immediate advice
      - When to seek medical help
      - Prevention measures
      - Local healthcare resources

2. ENTITY RECOGNITION REQUIREMENTS
   ================================

   Define entities for:
   - symptom: [fever, cough, pain, rash, etc.]
   - duration: [acute, chronic, days, weeks]
   - severity: [mild, moderate, severe]
   - age_group: [infant, child, adult, elderly]
   - location: [urban, rural, tribal]
   - gender: [male, female] (for gender-specific conditions)

3. RESPONSE TEMPLATES STRUCTURE
   ==============================

   Each response should include:
   
   A. Immediate Care Instructions
   B. Red Flag Symptoms (when to seek emergency care)
   C. Prevention Measures
   D. Local Healthcare Resources
   E. Follow-up Recommendations
   F. Cultural Sensitivity

4. REGIONAL CUSTOMIZATION
   =======================

   A. Coastal Districts: Higher malaria, cyclone-related health issues
   B. Tribal Areas: Malnutrition, traditional medicine integration
   C. Industrial Areas: Respiratory diseases, occupational health
   D. Urban Areas: Lifestyle diseases, mental health

5. SEASONAL PATTERNS
   ==================

   A. Monsoon (June-September): Vector-borne diseases, water-borne diseases
   B. Post-Monsoon (October-November): Dengue, chikungunya peak
   C. Winter (December-February): Respiratory infections
   D. Summer (March-May): Heat-related illnesses, dehydration
